Elite Intelligence & Merge Mechanics

1. The Merge Protocol

Enemies will naturally attempt to merge when they cluster in tight groups. This process transforms multiple normal units into a single powerful Elite entity.

  • Trigger: 10 units (or 5 Pentagons) within 100px.
  • Warmup: A 10-second visual resonance period before the merge completes.
  • Outcome: One unit consumes the others, fully restoring HP and gaining massive stat modifiers.

Advanced Game Mechanics

Enemy Scaling Logic

  • Spawn Rate: 1.4 + (0.1 × Minutes) per second.
    • Example @ 5 min: 1.7/sec
    • Example @ 10 min: 2.2/sec
  • Enemy HP: 50 × (1.15 ^ Minutes).
    • Scaling is compounding (+15% per minute).
    • Example @ 5 min: ~100 HP (2x)
    • Example @ 10 min: ~200 HP (4x)
    • Example @ 15 min: ~400 HP (8x)

Map Architecture & Arena Systems

1. Arena Grid (The Infinite Hex)

The world is divided into discrete Hexagonal Arenas. Crossing an Arena boundary shifts the gameplay environment and rules.

Arena Type Designation Bonus Property
Central Hub Sector 01 Standard: Default spawning and loot tables.
Economic Hex Resource Sector +15% XP Kill Bonus: Permanent buff while standing inside.
Fortress Hex High-Risk Sector Elite Saturation: Merges occur 20% faster; Elite spawns are common.

2. Quantum Reward Logic

Killing the Quantum Snitch triggers a world-state change for the next upgrade cycle.

  • XP Burst: Grants 100% of the current level's needed XP instantly.
  • Quantum Shift: The next upgrade slot is guaranteed to have vastly improved rarity percentages (Quantum+).

3. Navigational Limits

  • Void Boundaries: Hitting the map edge deals massive recoil damage (10% Max HP).
  • Snitch Recovery: The Real Snitch cannot be forced into the void. If pushed against a wall, it will use Ninja Smoke to teleport 800px toward the arena center.
